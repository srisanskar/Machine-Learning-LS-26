SPORTS @IITB
IIT Bombay Sports boasts of a rich culture, thriving on immense participation across 18 hostels and infrastructure most colleges can only dream of. Under its vast umbrella, there are sports at several levels: from Intra-hostel to Inter-hostel, and the crowning glory for every sportsperson at IIT Bombay, the Inter-IIT Sports meet. Aavhan is the Annual Sports Festival of IIT Bombay.
Sports offered include: Aquatics, Athletics, Badminton, Cricket, Table Tennis, Football, Board Games, Basketball, Lawn Tennis, Squash, Volleyball, Hockey.

CULTURALS @IITB
IIT Bombay undertakes a variety of cultural activities, broadly classified under 11 genres: Dance, Dramatics, Film and Media (F&M), Literary Arts, Music, Photography and Fine Arts (PFA), Speaking, Indian Languages, Lifestyle, Design, and Classical & Folk Arts. Activities include online quizzes, coding and hacking competitions, chess competitions, "cult school" for those wanting to pick up a new instrument or hone musical talents or learn a new language, campaigns for social causes, play reading sessions, summer school to develop acting skills, film/arts activities, and weekend musical concerts. Various clubs can be explored based on interest via the Gymkhana website (https://gymkhana.iitb.ac.in/~cultural) or the Institute's student clubs page (http://www.iitb.ac.in/en/activities/student-clubs).

LIBRARY AND COMMUTING FACILITIES
Teaching and research is supported by excellent computing facilities and libraries. The Central Library houses a large collection including old issues of magazines such as The Economist, The Economic and Political Weekly, and The Times Literary Supplement, along with theses, dissertations, and donated books. For commuting: the M-Indicator app is useful for finding your way around Mumbai, providing timings of local trains and buses along with auto and taxi fares. The nearest railway station to the IIT Bombay campus is Kanjurmarg, located on the Central Line of the Mumbai local train network. The Insti Map app can help navigate the campus itself.

STUDENT WELLNESS CENTRE (SWC)
"When we talk our feelings, it becomes less upsetting." It is not uncommon for students to feel burdened even after joining the institute due to growing demands and pressures. These issues can stem from academic concerns and social (family/peer) pressure, culminating in feelings of loneliness, low confidence, anxiety, stress, anger, and sadness. To help students cope with these challenges, the institute has a Student Wellness Centre with a team of professional counsellors who provide individual counselling and other facilities. Students can book an appointment via https://www.iitb.ac.in/swc/en/appointment or email wellness@iitb.ac.in. More details: http://www.iitb.ac.in/swc/en. Events and workshops are posted at http://www.facebook.com/icareiitb.

GENDER CELL
Previously known as the Women's Cell, the Gender Cell (GC) works proactively toward developing a safe and secure environment for students and employees, ensuring all students may pursue their education without fear of prejudice, gender bias, hostility, or sexual harassment. It has existed since 2002 and focuses on prevention, prohibition, and redress of sexual harassment. IIT Bombay's Gender Cell is an institutional body promoting equality, non-discrimination, and gender justice on campus. It enquires into complaints of sexual harassment through its Internal Complaints Committee (GC-ICC). The Gender Cell also conducts activities throughout the year including talks, movie screenings, and painting competitions. The team consists of faculty, student representatives (UG and PG level), and staff members.
Contact: Female QRT (Quick Response Team) in-charge, mail id - gendercell@iitb.ac.in. More info: https://www.gendercell.iitb.ac.in/team.html

THINGS TO REMEMBER (General Campus Tips)
- Always carry your ID Card.
- Download the M-Indicator app to find your way around Mumbai (local train/bus timings, auto/taxi fares).
- The nearest station to the IIT-B campus is Kanjurmarg, on the Central Line of the local train network.
- Download the Insti Map app to navigate the campus.
- Enjoy the sunset at the boathouse (lakeside), near Hostel 10, and the view of campus from Sameer Hill. Official visiting timings for these spots are 6 am - 6 pm. (Carry mosquito repellent.)
- For midnight hunger, every hostel has a canteen that usually opens in the evening and runs till 3 am.
- Useful Facebook groups: Freshmen Group, IITB Food Recommendations, Buy and Sell IITB, Hostel Groups, Official page of IIT Bombay.
- Join Google Groups of institute clubs (literary arts, performing arts, speaking arts, photography, etc.) as per your interests.
- Subscribe your official institute email ID to the Events mailing list to stay informed about campus events.
